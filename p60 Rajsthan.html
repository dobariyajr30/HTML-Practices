About Rajesthan

अजमेर-मेरवाडा प्रांत पर ब्रिटिश शासकों का कब्जा था; इस कारण यह तो सीधे ही स्वतंत्र भारत में आ जाती, मगर शेष इक्कीस रियासतों का विलय होना यानि एकीकरण कर 'राजस्थान' नामक प्रांत बनाया जाना था। सत्ता की होड़ के चलते यह बड़ा ही दूभर लग रहा था, क्योंकि इन देशी रियासतों के शासक अपनी रियासतों के स्वतंत्र भारत में विलय को दूसरी प्राथमिकता के रूप में देख रहे थे। उनकी मांग थी कि वे सालों से खुद अपने राज्यों का शासन चलाते आ रहे हैं, उन्हें इसका दीर्घकालीन अनुभव है, इस कारण उनकी रियासत को 'स्वतंत्र राज्य' का दर्जा दे दिया जाए। करीब एक दशक की ऊहापोह के बीच 18 मार्च 1948 को शुरू हुई राजस्थान के एकीकरण की प्रक्रिया कुल सात चरणों में एक नवंबर 1956 को पूरी हुई। जिसमे राजस्थान में 26 जिले सम्मिलित थे इसमें भारत सरकार के तत्कालीन देशी रियासत और गृह मंत्री सरदार वल्लभ भाई पटेल और उनके सचिव वी॰ पी॰ मेनन की भूमिका अत्यंत महत्वपूर्ण थी। इनकी सूझबूझ से ही राजस्थान के वर्तमान स्वरुप का निर्माण हो सका। राजस्थान में कुल 21 राष्ट्रीय राजमार्ग गुजरते हैं।


Cities of Rajesthan

Jaipur:-
Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan, is known as the "Pink City" due to its distinctive terracotta-colored buildings. With a population of over 3 million, Jaipur is a hub of historical and architectural wonders. The City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amer Fort showcase the grandeur of Rajput architecture. Jantar Mantar, an astronomical observatory, and Jal Mahal, a palace in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, are additional gems. The city's economy thrives on tourism, traditional crafts, and gemstone cutting.
Jodhpur:-
Famously known as the "Blue City" for its blue-painted houses, Jodhpur is the second-largest city in Rajasthan with a population exceeding 1.3 million. Dominated by the colossal Mehrangarh Fort, the city boasts architectural marvels like Umaid Bhawan Palace and Jaswant Thada. Mandore Gardens add a touch of natural beauty. Jodhpur's economy revolves around handicrafts, textiles, and a growing tourism industry.
Udaipur:-
Udaipur, often referred to as the "City of Lakes," is a picturesque city surrounded by the Aravalli Range. With a population of over 4 lakh, Udaipur is known for its enchanting lakes, including Lake Pichola and Fateh Sagar Lake. The City Palace, Jag Mandir, and Saheliyon Ki Bari are iconic landmarks. Udaipur's economy is driven by tourism, with its scenic beauty attracting visitors from around the world.
Jaisalmer:-
Jaisalmer, situated in the heart of the Thar Desert, is known as the "Golden City" due to its distinctive yellow sandstone architecture. The imposing Jaisalmer Fort, Patwon Ki Haveli, and Sam Sand Dunes are major attractions. With a population exceeding 78,000, Jaisalmer's economy relies on tourism, especially desert safaris and cultural experiences.
Rajkot:-
With a population exceeding 1.8 million, Rajkot is a vibrant city with attractions like Jubilee Garden and Watson Museum. It is an industrial center with a focus on automotive, engineering, and jewelry sectors.
Ajmer:-
Ajmer is a city with historical and religious significance, primarily known for the Dargah Sharif, the shrine of Sufi saint Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti. The Ana Sagar Lake, Taragarh Fort, and Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra are notable landmarks. Ajmer, with a population of over 5 lakh, is a key pilgrimage and cultural center.

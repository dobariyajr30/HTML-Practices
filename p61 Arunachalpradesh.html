Cities of Maharashtra

Hyderabad:-
Hyderabad, the capital and largest city of Andhra Pradesh (and Telangana), is a major IT and business hub. With a population of over 10 million, Hyderabad is known for its historic landmarks like the Charminar, Golconda Fort, and Qutb Shahi Tombs. The city is a major center for the information technology industry, housing HITEC City, and is also famous for its culinary delights, particularly the Hyderabadi Biryani.
Visakhapatnam:-
Visakhapatnam, often referred to as Vizag, is a coastal city with a population exceeding 2 million. It is a significant port city and an industrial hub. The city is known for its beautiful beaches, including RK Beach and Rushikonda Beach. Kailasagiri, INS Kurusura Submarine Museum, and Simhachalam Temple are key attractions. Visakhapatnam plays a crucial role in the state's economy with a focus on industries such as shipping, fishing, and steel manufacturing.
Vijayawada:-
Vijayawada, with a population of over 1 million, is a major commercial and industrial center. Situated on the banks of the Krishna River, the city is known for the Kanaka Durga Temple and Undavalli Caves. Vijayawada is a transportation hub and is well-connected by road, rail, and water. The city's economy is driven by trade, agriculture, and manufacturing..
Guntur:-
Guntur, with a population exceeding 8 lakh, is an important city in the agricultural heartland of Andhra Pradesh. It is renowned for its chili pepper cultivation and exports. Amaravati, the proposed capital city of Andhra Pradesh, is part of the Guntur Metropolitan Region. The city is also known for its historical sites like the Kondavid Fort and Amaravati Stupa.
Triputi:-
Tirupati is a sacred city known for the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in the world. With a population exceeding 4 lakh, Tirupati attracts millions of devotees annually. Besides religious tourism, the city is also known for its educational institutions and is an emerging economic center.
Warangal:-
Warangal, located in the northern part of Andhra Pradesh (now a part of Telangana), is a city with a rich historical heritage. With a population of over 8 lakh, it was the capital of the Kakatiya dynasty and is known for the Warangal Fort, Thousand Pillar Temple, and the Bhadrakali Temple. The city has a strong historical and cultural identity, attracting tourists interested in the region's architectural marvels. Warangal is also an emerging educational and industrial center, contributing to the economic growth of the area.
